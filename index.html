Saeed Iqbal, Ph.D.

Research Associate Professor

I am a Research Associate Professor in the College of Mechatronics and Control Engineering at Shenzhen University, China. Previously, I served as an Assistant Professor at the University of Central Punjab (UCP), Pakistan.

My research focuses on Federated Learning, Continual Learning, Machine Unlearning, and the computer-aided analysis of medical images. I aim to develop privacy-preserving, robust AI solutions that enhance diagnosis and prognosis in healthcare, bridging the gap between computational methods and clinical applications.

Selected Publications

Dynamic selectout and voting-based federated learning for enhanced medical image analysis
Saeed Iqbal, Adnan N. Qureshi, Musaed Alhussein, Khursheed Aurangzeb, Atif Mahmood, Saaidal Razalli Bin Azzuhri
Machine Learning: Science and Technology (IOP Science), 2025
Impact Factor: 6.3 Q1 HEC-W
PDF DOI

FedVoteNet introduces a voting mechanism and dynamic SelectOut within federated learning. By rewarding high-performing clients and selectively removing less effective ones, it improves adaptability to imbalanced medical imaging datasets.

Privacy-preserving AI for early diagnosis of thoracic diseases using IoTs: A federated learning approach
Imran Arshad Choudhry, Saeed Iqbal, Musaed Alhussein, Khursheed Aurangzeb, Adnan N. Qureshi, et al.
Internet of Things (Elsevier), 2024
Impact Factor: 6.0 Q1
PDF DOI

Introduces FedXNet, a federated learning-based deep learning model leveraging edge computing and Multi-Headed Self-Attention for early diagnosis of pulmonary abnormalities while preserving cross-institutional data privacy.

Hybrid Deep Spatial and Statistical Feature Fusion for Accurate MRI Brain Tumor Classification
Saeed Iqbal, Adnan N. Qureshi, Musaed Alhussein, Khursheed Aurangzeb, Imran Arshad Choudhry, et al.
Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience, 2024
Q2 F1 Score: 96.12%
PDF DOI

Introduces FusionNet, combining deep spatial characteristics with handcrafted statistical features using a ResNet-inspired fusion method, significantly outperforming existing methods on the BraTS dataset.

Hybrid Parallel Fuzzy CNN Paradigm: Unmasking Intricacies for Accurate Brain MRI Insights
Saeed Iqbal, Adnan N. Qureshi, Khursheed Aurangzeb, Musaed Alhussein, Shuihua Wang, et al.
IEEE Transactions on Fuzzy Systems, 2024
Impact Factor: 10.7 Q1 HEC-W
PDF DOI

HP-FCNN integrates fuzzy logic with CNNs to address noise and hazy borders in MRI brain images, achieving a 96.86 F1-Score and outperforming ResNet, DenseNet, and VGG.

Novel hybrid data-driven models for enhanced renewable energy prediction
Talal Alharbi, Saeed Iqbal
Frontiers in Energy Research, 2024
Impact Factor: 2.6 Q2
PDF DOI

Proposes the Solar Wind Energy Prediction System (SWEPS), utilizing PCA, RFE, LASSO, and genetic algorithms for feature selection, alongside neural networks to improve renewable energy forecast robustness.
